Electromagnetism

From static charges to the speed of light, electromagnetism weaves together electricity, magnetism, and radiation into a single theory.

Maxwell’s Equations

James Clerk Maxwell condensed decades of experiments into four equations: \[ \nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}, \quad \nabla \cdot \vec{B} = 0, \] \[ \nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}, \quad \nabla \times \vec{B} = \mu_0\vec{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial \vec{E}}{\partial t}. \] These laws explain how charges produce electric fields, how currents and changing electric fields generate magnetic fields, and why magnetism and electricity are inseparable.

Physical Meaning

Each equation tells a story: Gauss’s law relates charge to flux; Gauss’s law for magnetism states no magnetic monopoles exist; Faraday’s law shows that a changing magnetic field induces an electric field; Ampère–Maxwell’s law describes magnetic fields generated by both currents and time-varying electric fields. This framework unites electricity, magnetism, and optics.

The Lorentz Force

The Lorentz force law, \[ \vec{F} = q(\vec{E}+\vec{v}\times \vec{B}), \] defines how fields act on charges. It governs everything from electron beams in cathode ray tubes to particle trajectories in accelerators, linking abstract fields to tangible motion.

Electromagnetic Waves

Maxwell’s equations predict that disturbances in fields propagate as waves at speed \[ c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}}. \] These waves are transverse: electric and magnetic fields oscillate perpendicular to each other and the direction of propagation. Light, radio, X-rays, and all of the electromagnetic spectrum arise from this single fact.

Energy and Momentum

Fields carry energy and momentum. Energy density is \[ u=\tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2+\tfrac{1}{2\mu_0}B^2, \] while the Poynting vector \[ \vec{S}=\frac{1}{\mu_0}\vec{E}\times \vec{B} \] gives the flow of energy. Radiation pressure demonstrates that light can push matter, a principle used in solar sails and optical tweezers.

Radiation and Antennas

Accelerating charges radiate energy. Antennas exploit this by driving currents that emit electromagnetic waves. The Larmor formula, \[ P=\frac{\mu_0 q^2 a^2}{6\pi c}, \] predicts the power radiated by an accelerating charge, linking microscopic dynamics to global communication networks.

Relativity and Unification

Special relativity reveals that electric and magnetic fields are components of the same tensor \(F_{\mu\nu}\). A purely electric field in one frame may appear partly magnetic in another. This unification shows why the speed of light is universal and why electromagnetism is inherently relativistic.

Modern Significance

Electromagnetism powers modern life: motors, generators, fiber optics, lasers, satellites, and wireless communication. At the theoretical frontier, quantum electrodynamics builds on Maxwell’s framework and has been tested to twelve decimal places, making it one of science’s most precise achievements.
